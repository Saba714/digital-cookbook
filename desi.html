Desi Recipes

Chicken Karahi

Ingredients:

  • 1kg Chicken (pieces)
  • 5-6 Tomatoes (chopped)
  • Ginger & Garlic paste (2 tbsp)
  • Green Chilies & Fresh Coriander
  • Desi Ghee or Oil

Method:

1. Heat ghee in a wok (karahi) and fry the chicken until golden.
2. Add ginger-garlic paste and tomatoes.
3. Cook on high flame until the water dries and oil separates.
4. Garnish with green chilies and serve with hot Naan!

Beef Nihari

Ingredients:

  • 500g Beef Shank (Bong)
  • 1/2 cup Oil or Ghee
  • 3 tbsp Nihari Masala
  • 2 tbsp Wheat Flour (dissolved in water)
  • Ginger slivers & Lemon for garnish

Method:

1. Saute meat in oil with Nihari masala until browned.
2. Add water and slow-cook for 4-6 hours until meat is tender.
3. Slowly stir in the flour mixture to thicken the gravy.
4. Simmer for 15 minutes and serve with Ginger and Naan!

Sindhi Biryani

Ingredients:

  • 500g Basmati Rice
  • 500g Chicken & 2 Potatoes
  • 1 cup Yogurt
  • Dried Plums (Aloo Bukhara)
  • Biryani Spice Mix

Method:

1. Cook chicken and potatoes with yogurt and spices into a thick korma.
2. Boil rice until 70% cooked and drain the water.
3. Layer the rice over the korma and add food coloring.
4. Cover tightly (Dum) for 15 minutes on low heat until steam builds.

Shahi Tukray

Ingredients:

  • 4 slices White Bread
  • 1 Liter Milk
  • 1/2 cup Sugar
  • Cardamom powder & Saffron
  • Crushed Almonds & Pistachios

Method:

1. Fry bread triangles in ghee until deep golden and crispy.
2. Reduce milk by half by boiling it with sugar and cardamom.
3. Dip the fried bread in the thickened milk and arrange on a plate.
4. Pour remaining milk over and garnish with nuts.

Haleem

Ingredients:

  • 500g Beef or Chicken
  • 1 cup Wheat & Barley mix
  • 1/2 cup Mixed Lentils (Daal)
  • Fried onions & Chaat Masala
  • Oil and Haleem spices

Method:

1. Cook meat and grains separately until completely soft.
2. Mash or blend the mixture until it reaches a thick, silky consistency.
3. Temper with hot oil and fried onions (Tadka).
4. Serve with lemon, ginger, and fresh naan!
