Turkish Recipes

Adana Kebab

Ingredients:

  • 500g Ground Lamb (with fat)
  • 1 Red Bell Pepper (finely minced)
  • 1 tsp Chili Flakes (Pul Biber)
  • Salt and Black Pepper
  • Flatbread and Grilled Tomatoes

Method:

1. Mix the ground lamb with minced peppers and spices by hand.
2. Knead the mixture until it becomes a cohesive, sticky paste.
3. Thread the meat onto wide metal skewers, flattening it into a long shape.
4. Grill over hot coals until charred and juicy, then serve on flatbread!

Hünkar Beğendi (Sultan's Delight)

Ingredients:

  • 500g Lamb cubes
  • 2 large Eggplants
  • 1 cup Milk & 1 tbsp Flour
  • 1/2 cup Grated Kaşar cheese
  • Tomato paste and Butter

Method:

1. Slow-cook the lamb in a tomato-based sauce until tender.
2. Roast eggplants, peel them, and mash the flesh with flour, milk, and cheese.
3. Spread the creamy eggplant purée on a plate.
4. Top with the juicy lamb stew and serve warm!

Mercimek Köftesi

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup Red Lentils
  • 1/2 cup Fine Bulgur
  • 2 tbsp Tomato & Pepper paste
  • Fresh Parsley & Green Onions
  • Lettuce leaves for serving

Method:

1. Boil lentils until soft, then stir in bulgur and let it absorb the water.
2. Sauté onions with the pastes and mix into the lentil-bulgur mixture.
3. Add chopped herbs and shape into small bite-sized logs.
4. Serve with lettuce leaves and drizzle with lemon juice.!

Menemen

Ingredients:

  • 3 Eggs
  • 2 Tomatoes (peeled and diced)
  • 2 Green Peppers (Sivri Biber)
  • 2 tbsp Butter
  • Red pepper flakes

Method:

1. Sauté the peppers in butter until soft.
2. Add tomatoes and cook until they break down into a sauce.
3. Crack the eggs directly into the pan and stir gently.
4. Keep the eggs slightly creamy and serve with crusty bread!

Baklava

Ingredients:

  • 1 pack Phyllo pastry
  • 2 cups Crushed Pistachios
  • 1 cup Melted Butter
  • 2 cups Sugar & 1 cup Water
  • 1 tsp Lemon juice

Method:

1. Layer phyllo sheets in a tray, brushing each with butter.
2. Spread a thick layer of pistachios in the middle.
3. Layer remaining sheets, cut into diamonds, and bake until golden.
4. Pour cooled sugar syrup over the hot baklava and let it soak!
